Marrakesh places

01

Menara

The Menara Gardens Marrakech in the Maghreb is one of the most wonderful natural attractions in Marrakesh and is also one of the most beautiful distinctive gardens in the Kingdom of Morocco. The Manara Garden in Marrakech has beautiful green spaces and more than wonderful fresh water bodies. The lighthouse is located in Marrakech near the green Atlas Mountains that are charming to the eyes.

02

Jamaâ El Fna

Jamaâ El Fna Square is a popular space for watching and recreation for locals and tourists in Marrakesh, Morocco. Accordingly, this square is the beating heart of Marrakesh, where it was and still is a meeting point between the city and the Kasbah of the Makhzen and the Mellah, and a pilgrimage for visitors from all over the world to enjoy watching interesting performances of snake tamers. Telling riddles and stories.

03

Majorelle Garden

The Majorelle Gardens are located in Marrakesh, in Gueliz. The Majorelle is named after its builder, the French painter Jacques Majorelle, who began its founding in 1924.

04

Koutoubia Mosque

The Koutoubia Marrakchi Mosque is one of the well-established Islamic monuments in the history of Morocco. The Koutoubia Mosque is located in Marrakesh, near Jamaâ El Fna Square. The name of the mosque derives from the "Kitabien", which is the name of a book-selling market believed to have been close to mosques.

05

Bahia Palace

Bahia Palace is one of the historical monuments of the Moroccan city of Marrakesh. Bahia Palace located in Marrakesh, whose construction dates back to the era of the Alawite state of Minister Ahmed bin Musa during the reign of Sultan Abdelaziz, the director of state affairs at the time, nicknamed “Ba Hammad”, where the latter brought the most skilled craftsmen and craftsmen to work in the palace for six consecutive years, except The inevitable fate prevented him from seeing the palace when he passed away in 1900 and the work was not finished yet. It is one of the most famous historical monuments in Marrakesh.

06

City commercial markets

The commercial markets in the old city are considered one of the most important places of tourism in Marrakech and the most famous shopping destination in the city, where the old city is characterized by its narrow alleys and colorful houses. The area offers many shops that sell distinctive souvenirs such as shoes, perfumes, copper antiques, etc. There is also a market dedicated to leather, where many old tanneries are located in the area.

07

Ben Youssef School

The Ali Ben Youssef School is one of the best places of tourism in Marrakesh. It is considered a historical architectural masterpiece. It was founded in 1346 AD and was home to great scholars and writers who taught religious sciences, jurisprudence and other sciences, so it is considered one of the most famous historical schools in Morocco. The school today is one of the most important tourist places in Marrakech. It attracts tourists as it is an archaeological masterpiece that reflects the prosperity of Moroccan civilization.

08

Almoravid dome

The Almoravid dome is considered the oldest tourist place in Marrakesh, dating back to 1064. It was built during the reign of Ali bin Youssef as a ablution house for worshipers in the Ben Youssef Mosque. The dome reflects the art of Almoravid architecture through its solid construction that is not affected by the fluctuations of time, in addition to the water delivery system to it, which is unique in that the water passes through it from underground to pour into bronze pipes.

09

Marrakesh wall

This wall is considered one of the most important tourist places in Marrakech. Its construction dates back to the year 1126 AD and was built with the aim of protecting the city from the enemies. The rammed earth was used to build the wall, which still stands today, without being affected by the fluctuations of time. The Marrakesh Wall is a unique architectural masterpiece, extending 9 km in length to surround the city.

10

Marrakesh Museums

The museums of Marrakesh represent the civilized interface of the city, as it introduces visitors to its historical and cultural heritage, and this is what motivates a large number of visitors to search for the best museums in Marrakesh, as it is one of the most important tourist places in Marrakesh that you can not miss.

11

Marrakesh Gardens

Most visitors take the Marrakesh Gardens as a symbol of the beauty of this city and as one of the tourist areas in Marrakech that cannot be missed. This article will explain the most beautiful gardens in Marrakech that we invite nature lovers to visit.

12

Dar Si Said Museum

Dar Si Said Museum is located in the Moroccan city of Marrakesh. One of the luxurious traditional houses in Marrakesh, which was established in the second half of the nineteenth century by Said bin Musa, who was exercising the duties of Minister of War during the reign of Sultan Moulay Abdel Aziz. He was also the brother of Ahmed bin Musa Al Sharqi, chief minister of the Sultan at the time, known as Bahamad.

13

Arsat Moulay Abdel Salam

Arsat Moulay Abdelsalam is a cultural and historical park located in the center of Marrakesh, full of diverse plants and 100-year-old trees. It is the first park fully connected to the Internet with high throughput in Morocco.

14

Saadian tomb

The mausoleum of the honorable Saadians is one of the most famous shrines in Morocco and one of the historical monuments of the city of Marrakesh. It is located in the Ksaiba and was closed until the time of protection, when it was discovered as a historical landmark. It was built in 1557 AD by Sultan Abdullah Al-Ghalib and expanded by Sultan Ahmed Al-Mansour. This mausoleum houses most of the Saadian sultans and their families.
